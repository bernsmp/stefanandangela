## SECTION 4: COMMUNICATION PREFERENCES

### SELF-REPORTED (How She Wants to Be Communicated With)

**Best Practice (Her Prescription):**
1. TL;DR in 1-2 sentences ("Here's the situation + impact")
2. Problem + proposed solution in one breath
3. What's needed from her ("I need your yes/no on this solution")
4. Optional detail only if she asks

**Examples She'd Love:**
> "We had a data issue: X happened, Y is affected. I recommend we do A and B by Friday. Any objections?"
> 
> "We're behind on [initiative], but I've mapped a 3-step adjustment so we hit the target anyway. Want the 60-second version?"

**What Not To Do:**
- Long, rambling preambles
- Explaining entire process step-by-step
- Over-indexing on feelings before outcomes
- Tracking things "because we've always tracked them"
- Bringing raw problems without solution concepts
- Asking permission for every micro-step

## SECTION 9: STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS

### Where Self-Awareness Helps

Angela's conscious awareness of her communication preferences, energizers, and core philosophy means:
- She can articulate what she needs from team
- She can design her work environment deliberately
- She can communicate her expectations clearly
- Team can adapt to her known preferences

### Where Lack of Self-Awareness Creates Risk

The 55% of patterns running unconsciously means:
- She can't teach these patterns systematically (they feel like "common sense")
- Blind spots operate without correction
- Team depends on her presence for patterns she doesn't know she runs
- Scaling is limited by her personal capacity

### Key Intervention Points

1. **Make Quantification Reflex Visible** - Create "proceed without numbers" categories
2. **Surface Training Debt** - Add 3-minute "why this matters" to delegations
3. **Create Exploration Budget** - 10% of attention on non-decision-utility tracking
4. **Document Unconscious Patterns** - Convert the 11 unconscious patterns to teachable methods
